C10: Savings, Investment Spending, and the Financial System

  • Savings-Investment Spending Identity in a Closed Economy
    • $$GDP=C+I+G+X-IM$$
    • $$GDP=C+I+G$$ (Total income = Total spending)
    • $$GDP=C+G+S$$ (Total income = Consumption Spending + Savings)
    • $$GDP=C+G+I$$ (Total income = Consumption Spending + Investment spending)
    • $$C+G+S=C+G+I$$ (Consumption spending + Savings = Consumption spending + Investment spending)
    • $$S=I$$ (Savings = Investment Spending)
    • Government Savings $$S_G=T-G-TR$$
    • National Savings: $$S_N=S_G+S_P$$
    • $$S_N=I$$ is the Savings-Investment Spending Identity
  • Savings-Investment Spending Identity in an Open Economy
    • $$NCI=IM-X$$ (Net Capital Inflow = Imports - Exports)
    • $$I=S_N+(IM-X)=S_N+NCI$$
  • The Market for Loanable Funds
    • The sign for the Interest Rate is $$r$$
    • Demand for Loanable Funds
      • Y = Interest rate
      • X = Q of Loanable Funds
      • Downward slope to right
    • Supply of Loanable Funds
      • Y = Interest rate
      • X = Q of Loanable Funds
      • Upward slope to right
    • The Equilibrium Interest Rate
    • Shifts of the Demand for Loanable Funds
      • Changes in perceived business opportunities
      • Changes in government borrowing
    • Shifts of the Supply of Loanable Funds
      • Changes in private savings behavior
      • Changes in net capital inflows
